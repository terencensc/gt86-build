The Mission

Build a Group 3E-compliant Toyota GT86 for Australian endurance racing. The goal: maximum track time at minimum cost, learn to drive fast, have fun, and get Ringwood Service Centre some exposure while we're at it. We're not here to win — we're here to race.

Recommended Path Best Fit

Australian Production Cars (APC) Enduro Championship — 2-3 hour endurance races, Group 3E technical rules, up to 3 drivers per car, team branding allowed. The GT86 slots into Class D (Production Cars) with a minimum weight of 1179-1218kg depending on variant. Runs under AASA at circuits across Australia.

This is your primary target series. Endurance format gives the most seat time per dollar, and you can advertise Ringwood Service Centre on the car.

Recommended Strategy

Phase 1 (Months 1-6): Build car to Group 3E spec. Get second driver licensed. Run MSCA sprint days for car shakedown and driver practice.

Phase 2 (Months 6-9): Enter IPRA VIC rounds for affordable state-level racing. Build race experience and get provisional endorsement removed.

Phase 3 (Months 9-12+): Enter APC Enduro Championship for maximum seat time. This is the main target — 2-3hr endurance races with team branding.

Dream Goal (Year 2+): Bathurst 6 Hour once budget allows and both drivers have non-provisional licences.

Licence Pathway

Driver 1 — Has Existing Licence

One driver already holds a competition licence. Verify it covers Circuit Race discipline and check if it's a Motorsport Australia or AASA licence. For APC, you'll need an AASA National Racing Licence ($570/yr). For TGRA/Bathurst, you'll need a Motorsport Australia Circuit Licence ($556/yr).

If holding a Motorsport Australia licence with provisional endorsement: Bathurst 6 Hour is NOT eligible (no provisional allowed at Mt Panorama). APC under AASA may have different rules — confirm with AASA.

Driver 2 — Needs New Licence

Here's the pathway to get race-licensed:

Step 1
Medical Examination
Visit a doctor for Motorsport Australia Medical Standards assessment. Required before licence application.
Step 2
Join a Motorsport Australia-affiliated club
Must be a member of an affiliated car club (not just an Enthusiast Club). Examples: ARDC, IPRA VIC, local car racing club.
Step 3
Pre-Licence Lecture
Complete the Motorsport Australia Circuit Race Licence Lecture (available online). Must be completed before OLT.
Step 4
Observed Licence Test (OLT)
On-track assessment by approved OLT provider. Driving style, ability, and attitude evaluated. Must be completed within 1 year of the lecture. Costs vary (~$300-600).
Step 5
Licence Issued — Circuit with Provisional Endorsement
Initial licence comes with Provisional endorsement (P-plate displayed on car). Cost: ~$556/yr for Motorsport Australia. Min age: 15 for Toyota 86 (16 at Bathurst).
Step 6 (upgrade)
Remove Provisional Endorsement
Compete to the satisfaction of Clerk of Course at 3 Motorsport Australia Circuit Race Events in the previous 24 months. Passbook signed by Clerk of Course.
For AASA licence (APC): Separate application at aasa.com.au. National Racing Licence costs ~$570/yr. Different pathway but similar concept. Check if AASA accepts Motorsport Australia licence holders.
